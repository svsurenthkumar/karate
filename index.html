Born to Fight · Built to Inspire

From Fisherman's Son to Global Grandmaster

Born on 10 August 1964 into a humble fisherman's family in Neelangarai, Chennai, Sekar discovered karate at age 12. Inspired by Bruce Lee, he trained up to eight hours daily — shaping an iron will that would define his life.

He won three national championships (1984–86), set a world record sparring 200 black belts continuously, and became the first and youngest Indian to receive an 8th Dan directly from an Okinawan grandmaster — Kaicho Dr. Tetsuhiro Hokama.

In 2024, after a life-saving liver transplant (his daughter Sinthiya donated 70% of her liver), Sekar returned to karate training within six months — embodying the warrior spirit he has taught for decades.

The Path of a Warrior

Milestones & Battles

1976
The Spark
Age 12. A fisherman's son begins training in karate. Eight hours of daily practice becomes his religion.
1984–86
National Champion
Three consecutive national championship victories cement his reputation as one of India's finest fighters.
1993
Bronze Through Injury
Wins a national bronze medal with a dislocated knee. Major surgery and six months of rehabilitation follow.
1996
World Record
Continuously spars 200 black belts without a break — a feat witnessed and honoured by the Joint Commissioner of Police.
2001
Heavyweight Gold
Powerful comeback at the RVT Mani Memorial Trophy. Then searches for the true Okinawan masters.
2003
World Championship · Okinawa
Places 8th in Kobudo at the World Championship in Okinawa. Restarts as a white belt under Kaicho Dr. Hokama.
2007
Hokama World Cup
Personally sponsors and organises the Hokama World Cup — 3,000 competitors from 8+ nations, Chennai.
2013
8th Dan · History Made
Youngest and first Indian to receive 8th Dan directly from an Okinawan master. Asian Chief Coach of Kenshikai.
2023–24
Triumph Over Death
Receives 9th Dan in Okinawa. Undergoes life-saving liver transplant. Returns to karate in six months. True warrior.
2025
Board of Directors · Kenshikai
Appointed to the Board of Directors of the International Kenshikai Goju-Ryu Karate and Kobudo Organization.

The Dojo Network

Training. Discipline. Service.

01 🥋
SMAC Head Dojo

4,700 sq. ft. fully equipped facility in Chennai with professional safety mats, dedicated kobudo (weapons) training areas, and state-of-the-art training infrastructure.

02 🌏
India-Wide Branches

From a single dojo in Neelangarai in 2004, SMAC has grown to multiple branches across India, now training nearly 2,000 students in the authentic Goju-Ryu tradition.

03 🙏
Free Training for All

Rooted in service — SMAC provides high-quality karate training to poor and deserving students, with completely free coaching for those who cannot afford the fees.

04 ⚔️
Kobudo & Weapons

One of India's few institutions offering authentic Okinawan Kobudo (weapons) training under direct lineage from Kaicho Dr. Tetsuhiro Hokama, Japan.

05 🏆
Championship Factory

All five of Hanshi Sekar's children have won national gold medals and represented India internationally — a family legacy unique in the history of Indian karate.

06 🌐
International Affiliation

Officially affiliated with the International Kenshikai Goju-Ryu Karate and Kobudo Organization, Okinawa, Japan — one of the most respected karate bodies in the world.
